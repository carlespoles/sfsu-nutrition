Why Does This Matter?

College students face unique barriers to healthy eating. Understanding the problem is the first step toward change.

🍟

Ultra-Processed Food Overload

Convenience foods high in sodium, sugar, and refined carbohydrates dominate the college diet, contributing to weight gain, fatigue, and poor academic focus.

💸

Budget Constraints

Students often rely on cheap, calorie-dense processed options because healthy food is perceived — and sometimes genuinely is — more expensive and time-consuming.

📚

Nutrition Knowledge Gap

Most undergraduate students receive little to no formal nutrition education in their degree programs, leaving them without tools to make informed food decisions.

🏙️

Environmental Barriers

Campus and neighborhood food environments often make processed foods the path of least resistance, reinforcing poor dietary habits throughout the academic year.

Program Curriculum

Eight evidence-based modules delivered across one semester, blending online content with hands-on in-person workshops.

Weeks 1–2

📖 What Is Processed Food?

Understanding the NOVA classification system, reading nutrition labels, and identifying ultra-processed foods in everyday diets.

  • NOVA food classification
  • Label reading activity
  • Personal food log baseline
Weeks 3–4

🧠 Food & Brain Performance

How dietary patterns affect cognitive function, memory, concentration, and academic performance in college students.

  • Gut-brain connection
  • Energy fluctuations & sugar crashes
  • Study snack planning
Weeks 5–6

🛒 Smart Grocery Shopping

How to build a weekly meal plan, navigate grocery stores strategically, and maximize nutrition on a limited student budget.

  • Budget meal planning
  • Seasonal produce guide
  • Clement St. Farmers Market tour
Weeks 7–8

🍳 Cooking Skills Basics

Hands-on cooking demonstrations showing how simple whole food meals can be prepared in 20 minutes or less with minimal equipment.

  • Batch cooking techniques
  • 5-ingredient whole food recipes
  • Dorm-friendly meal prep
Weeks 9–10

🌍 Food Systems & PSE

How policies, systems, and environments shape what we eat — and how students can advocate for healthier campus food environments.

  • Food desert mapping activity
  • SFSU campus food audit
  • Advocacy letter writing
Weeks 11–12

🧘 Mindful Eating

Developing a healthy relationship with food, recognizing emotional eating patterns, and building sustainable dietary habits without restriction.

  • Hunger vs. craving awareness
  • Stress eating strategies
  • Non-diet approach principles
Weeks 13–14

🏷️ Navigating Food Marketing

Recognizing deceptive health claims, understanding front-of-package marketing, and becoming a critical consumer of nutrition information.

  • Deconstructing health halos
  • "Natural" vs. nutritious
  • Social media & diet culture
Weeks 15–16

🎯 Goal Setting & Reflection

Students review their personal food logs, set SMART nutrition goals for the next semester, and share program outcomes with peers.

  • Pre/post dietary assessment
  • SMART goal framework
  • Peer accountability pairs

Processed vs. Whole Foods

Understanding the difference is the foundation of this program. Here's a quick comparison to get you started.

🚫 Ultra-Processed Foods

  • Packaged chips, cookies, crackers
  • Instant ramen & cup noodles
  • Energy drinks & sodas
  • Fast food & frozen meals
  • Flavored yogurts & cereals
  • Deli meats & hot dogs

High in sodium, added sugar, saturated fat, and artificial additives. Linked to inflammation, fatigue, and chronic disease risk.

✅ Whole & Minimally Processed Foods

  • Fresh fruits & vegetables
  • Brown rice, oats, quinoa
  • Beans, lentils, chickpeas
  • Eggs, plain Greek yogurt
  • Nuts, seeds, nut butters
  • Canned tomatoes, frozen veggies

Rich in fiber, vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants. Support energy, focus, immune function, and long-term health.

Quick Tips for Students

Small, practical changes that make a real difference — no perfection required.

🫙

Batch Cook on Sundays

Prep a big pot of rice, beans, or lentils once a week. It costs under $5 and feeds you for days.

🧃

Swap Sugary Drinks

Replace sodas and energy drinks with water, sparkling water, or unsweetened tea. Save money and reduce sugar intake instantly.

🍎

Keep Fruit Visible

Place apples, bananas, or oranges on your desk or bag. You're more likely to eat what you can see.

🥫

Stock Canned Goods

Canned beans, tomatoes, and tuna are cheap, nutritious, and shelf-stable — perfect for busy student life.

🛒

Shop the Perimeter

Whole foods — produce, dairy, meat — line the outer edges of most grocery stores. The interior aisles are where most ultra-processed foods live.

📱

Use CalFresh (SNAP)

If you're income-eligible, CalFresh can provide $200+ per month for groceries. Apply at GetCalFresh.org — many SFSU students qualify.
